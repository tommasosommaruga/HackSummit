How the score is calculated
Scoring Methodology
// Step 1 — Per-signal weight
W(signal) = Severity × Confidence × (1 − Deniability)

// Step 2 — Dimension score (Bayesian non-independence product)
Score(dimension) = 1 − ∏[1 − W(s)] for all signals s in dimension

// Step 3 — Composite score across three independent risk dimensions
Composite = 1 − (1 − Score[ChildLabour]) × (1 − Score[ForcedLabour]) × (1 − Score[DocFraud])

Why three dimensions?

Child labour, forced labour, and document fraud are analytically distinct risk types, each driven by different causal mechanisms and detectable via different data sources. A site can exhibit high child labour risk but low fraud risk (artisanal mine, no paperwork at all) or the reverse (industrial site with well-documented but forged certificates). The composite fuses them so that evidence in any dimension lifts the overall flag.

Why Bayesian product, not average?

Averaging signals would dilute the impact of one very strong indicator. The product formula models independent risk events: if child-labour risk is 85% and forced-labour risk is 70%, the composite is 1 − (0.15 × 0.30) = 95.5%, not (85+70)/2 = 77.5%. This reflects the investigative reality that two independent risk types pointing at the same site are far more alarming than one risk scored twice.

Deniability — the critical variable

Deniability scores how easily an operator can explain away a signal with a legitimate reason. A production spike could be explained by a new equipment delivery (high deniability). A certificate dated before the mine permit exists has zero deniability — it is a logical impossibility. Signals with low deniability receive disproportionate weight even at moderate severity.

Thresholds for investigation referral

0–30% — Low. Standard monitoring cadence.
30–55% — Moderate. Enhanced due diligence required.
55–75% — High. Field investigation recommended.
75%+ — Critical. Suspend sourcing pending audit. Report to OECD/ILO.

For Investigators
How to Use This Score in Practice
TOP-DOWN APPROACH

Starting from a finished product

You have a battery pack or REE magnet and want to trace its material back to origin. Use the product's EU Battery Passport ID, SEC Form SD filing, or company sustainability report to identify declared refiners. Cross-reference refiners against the RMI Compliant Smelter list. For each declared upstream supplier, run a ChainTrace score on the corresponding mining region. Discrepancies between declared and scored risk indicate where to focus field investigation.

BOTTOM-UP APPROACH

Starting from a mine or country

You have intelligence or an alert from a mine region. Run the regional score first. High-scoring regions identify the most likely risk nodes. Then trace forward: which refiners source from this region (UN Comtrade HS code + bilateral flows), which OEMs buy from those refiners (SEC EDGAR, company disclosures), which products end up in which markets. The score tells you how urgently to pursue each forward hop.

MISSING DATA AS A SIGNAL

Absence of information is evidence

If a mine has no audit record, no EITI disclosure, no ILO country report match, and no satellite coverage, that absence itself elevates risk. The scoring engine adds a +10% base penalty for regions with fewer than 3 data sources available. An operator who makes verification impossible is exercising opacity for a reason.

COMPOUND SIGNAL LOGIC

Weak signals that cluster are strong signals

No single weak signal (severity < 50) triggers an investigation alone. But the Bayesian product means three weak signals at 40% severity each produce a dimension score of 78%. The engine surfaces compound patterns automatically — an investigator should read the per-dimension signal list, not just the composite number, to understand which cluster of evidence is driving the score.
